1. Device Identification and Intended Purpose

Device Name: ThermaScan PRO-2000
Device Classification: Class IIa per MDR 2017/745 Annex VIII Rule 10
Intended Purpose: Intermittent measurement and monitoring of human body temperature via the ear canal (tympanic membrane) in adult and pediatric patients aged 3 months and above, in clinical and home healthcare settings.
Target Population: Adults, children, and infants aged 3 months and above. Not indicated for neonates under 3 months.
Contraindications: Patients with ear canal obstruction, severe otitis externa, or recent ear surgery.
Clinical Benefits:
- B1: Rapid non-invasive temperature measurement within 1 second, enabling early fever detection.
- B2: Elimination of invasive temperature measurement methods (rectal, esophageal) in routine practice.
- B3: Infection control through single-use disposable probe covers.
- B4: Memory function (30 readings) and fever alert for clinical usability.

6. Equivalence Demonstration

This CER relies on equivalence to the Braun ThermaScan PRO 6000 (predicate device, CE marked under MDD 93/42/EEC since 2014) per MDCG 2020-5:

Technical Equivalence: Both devices use identical infrared thermopile detector technology (wavelength 8-14 micrometers), same measurement range (34.0-42.2 degrees C), and comparable calibration algorithms. The ergonomic probe design, single-use probe cover system, and LCD display interface are functionally equivalent. Source: CER_POSITIVE:Section6:TechnicalEquivalence

Biological Equivalence: Both devices target the same anatomical site (tympanic membrane via ear canal). The tissue interaction (passive infrared radiation absorption) is identical. No differences in measurement physiology exist between the two devices. Source: CER_POSITIVE:Section6:BiologicalEquivalence

Clinical Equivalence: Both devices share identical intended purpose, target populations, clinical settings, and operating conditions. Clinical performance data for the predicate device are applicable to the subject device. Source: CER_POSITIVE:Section6:ClinicalEquivalence

Data Access: The manufacturer confirms access to predicate device technical documentation and clinical data per MDCG 2020-5 Section 4.1. A Data Access Agreement is on file (DOC-DAA-2026-001).

Limitation: This equivalence demonstration relies on literature review and predicate device post-market data. No head-to-head comparative clinical investigation was conducted between the subject device and the predicate device. This limitation is noted and disclosed.
